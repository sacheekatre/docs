Closd is designed to make legal transactions simpler, faster, and more secure — but getting started can raise questions. Whether you're a first-time user signing a document or a deal team managing a complex M&A transaction, this page covers the most common questions we hear from legal professionals using the platform.

<AccordionGroup>

  {/* ── Account & Access ─────────────────────────────────────────── */}

  <Accordion title="Do I need a Closd account to sign a document?">
    No — you can sign a document in Closd without creating an account. When a signature request is sent to you, you'll receive an email with a secure link that lets you review and sign directly in your browser. If you want to manage your own projects, send signature requests, or access a data room, you will need a Closd account. Your project administrator can invite you by email.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How many users can I invite to a project?">
    Closd does not impose a fixed cap on the number of users per project — your subscription plan determines the overall user limits. This makes Closd well-suited for large deal teams involving multiple law firms, advisors, and counterparties. For specific limits tied to your plan, contact your Closd account manager or check your subscription settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can external parties — counterparties, advisors, or clients — access my project?">
    Yes. Closd is built for multi-party transactions, so you can invite external participants — including opposing counsel, financial advisors, and clients — directly to your project. Each invitee receives only the permissions you grant them; you control exactly which folders, documents, and features they can see. External users do not need to be on a paid Closd plan to accept an invitation and collaborate.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my data if I cancel my subscription?">
    If you cancel your Closd subscription, you retain the ability to export your documents and project data during a defined grace period before the account is closed. Closd recommends downloading your closing binders and archiving key documents before cancellation. For details on data retention and deletion timelines, review your subscription agreement or contact Closd support.
  </Accordion>

  {/* ── Projects & Documents ─────────────────────────────────────── */}

  <Accordion title="Can I use Closd for multiple deals at the same time?">
    Yes — Closd is designed for concurrent deal management. You can create and run multiple projects simultaneously, each with its own checklist, document room, signature workflow, and team members. Switching between projects is instant from your Closd dashboard. This is especially useful for law firms and in-house teams managing several transactions at once.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What file types can I upload to Closd?">
    Closd supports all common business document formats, including PDF, Microsoft Word (.doc, .docx), Excel (.xls, .xlsx), PowerPoint (.ppt, .pptx), and image files. PDF is the recommended format for documents that will go through the e-signature workflow, as it preserves formatting across all devices. If you need to upload a file type not listed here, contact Closd support to confirm compatibility.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is there a storage limit on my project?">
    Storage limits depend on your Closd subscription plan. Most plans are designed to handle the volume of documents typical in complex transactions — including large data rooms with hundreds of files. If you anticipate uploading an unusually large volume of data, speak with your Closd account manager about your plan's capacity. Individual file uploads are limited to 100 MB per file.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I restore a deleted document?">
    Closd maintains version history and, depending on your plan and configuration, may retain recently deleted files for a short recovery window. If you have accidentally deleted a document, contact your project administrator or Closd support as quickly as possible. To avoid accidental deletion, consider using Closd's access control settings to restrict delete permissions to project administrators only.
  </Accordion>

  {/* ── E-Signature ──────────────────────────────────────────────── */}

  <Accordion title="Are Closd electronic signatures legally valid?">
    Yes. Closd's electronic signatures are legally valid across the European Union and in many other jurisdictions. Closd supports Simple, Advanced, and Qualified Electronic Signatures (QES) in compliance with the EU eIDAS Regulation, which gives Qualified signatures the same legal standing as handwritten signatures under EU law. Each signed document is accompanied by an audit trail and a signing certificate that provides evidentiary proof of the transaction.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What is the difference between DocuSign and CertEurope in Closd?">
    Closd integrates with two signature providers — DocuSign and CertEurope — so you can choose the one that best fits your transaction and jurisdictional requirements. DocuSign is widely recognized globally and is a strong choice for cross-border deals. CertEurope is a French-qualified trust service provider (TSP) under eIDAS, making it particularly well-suited for transactions requiring Qualified Electronic Signatures in the EU. Your Closd administrator can configure the preferred provider at the project level.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can signers sign documents on a mobile device?">
    Yes — signers can open signature request links and complete signing on smartphones and tablets using any modern mobile browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge). No app installation is required. If a signer experiences difficulty on mobile, advise them to try the same link on a desktop or laptop browser as a fallback, particularly for Qualified signature workflows that may require identity verification steps.
  </Accordion>

  {/* ── Security & Compliance ────────────────────────────────────── */}

  <Accordion title="Where is my data stored?">
    All Closd data is stored exclusively on servers located within the European Union. Closd does not transfer your data outside the EU, which is a key consideration for legal professionals subject to EU data protection obligations. This European data residency policy applies to your documents, project metadata, audit trails, and archived files.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Is Closd GDPR compliant?">
    Yes. Closd is fully compliant with the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). As a data processor, Closd has implemented the technical and organisational measures required by GDPR, and a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available for enterprise customers upon request. You retain full control over the personal data stored in your projects and can request data deletion in accordance with your rights under GDPR.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What security certifications does Closd hold?">
    Closd is owned by LexisNexis, part of the RELX Group, and operates within a rigorous enterprise security framework. Closd's long-term archiving partner, Arkhineo, holds certifications including NF Z42-013 (the French standard for trusted archiving) and operates in accordance with eIDAS trust service requirements. For the most current list of certifications and audit reports applicable to your organisation, contact Closd directly to request their Security Whitepaper.
  </Accordion>
