Most issues in Closd can be resolved quickly with a few targeted steps. This guide covers the problems users encounter most often — from login difficulties and document upload errors to e-signature failures — and walks you through how to fix them. If you work through the relevant steps and the problem persists, the Closd support team is available to help directly.

## Login & Access Issues

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="I can't log in to my account">
    Start with the basics: check that Caps Lock is not on and that you're entering the email address associated with your Closd account. If you've forgotten your password, use the **Forgot Password** link on the login page at [app.closd.com](https://app.closd.com) to receive a reset email. If the reset email doesn't arrive within a few minutes, check your spam or junk folder. If your account was created by an administrator (for example, you were invited to a project by your firm), contact that administrator to confirm your account is active and has not been deactivated.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I didn't receive my invitation email">
    Invitation emails from Closd can sometimes be filtered by spam or junk mail folders — check those first. If the email isn't there, ask the project administrator to resend the invitation from the project's **Team** settings. Verify with your administrator that the invitation was sent to the correct email address; if there was a typo, they will need to remove the incorrect address and re-invite you with the right one. Some corporate email security gateways may also block external invitation links — ask your IT team to allowlist emails from Closd if this is a recurring issue.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I'm getting 'Access Denied' when opening a project or document">
    An "Access Denied" message means your user account has not been granted permission to view that project or specific document. Contact the project administrator and ask them to check your access rights in the project's **Team** or **Permissions** settings. It's possible your permissions were changed, your invitation expired before you accepted it, or the project has been restricted to a different group of participants. Your administrator can update your permissions or re-invite you as needed.

    <Warning>
      Do not attempt to access a project or document by reusing a link that was shared with another participant. Each user's access is tied to their own authenticated session, and using someone else's link will result in an access error or security block.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Document Issues

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="My document upload is failing">
    Before retrying, check the following:

    - **File size:** Closd supports uploads up to **100 MB per file**. If your file exceeds this limit, compress it or split it into smaller parts before uploading.
    - **File format:** Supported formats include PDF, Word (.doc, .docx), Excel (.xls, .xlsx), PowerPoint (.ppt, .pptx), and common image formats. Files in unsupported formats will be rejected.
    - **Browser:** Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge are recommended). Make sure your browser is up to date.
    - **Cache:** Clear your browser cache and cookies, then try the upload again.

    If uploads continue to fail after these steps, try from a different network (for example, disable your VPN if one is active) and contact Closd support if the issue persists.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I uploaded the wrong version of a document">
    You can replace a document without losing the history. Right-click the document in your project folder and select **Upload New Version**. Closd will store the previous version in the document's version history, which remains accessible to users with the appropriate permissions. This keeps your audit trail intact and ensures all participants are working from the latest version without permanently discarding earlier drafts.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A document is showing as corrupted or won't open">
    A corrupted file display is usually caused by an issue with the original file rather than the Closd platform itself. Try downloading the file and opening it locally — if it opens correctly, the issue may be browser-related; try a different browser or clear your cache. If the local file is also corrupted, re-upload the original source document. If the problem persists after re-uploading a clean file, contact Closd support and provide the project name and document details so they can investigate.
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## E-Signature Issues

<AccordionGroup>

  <Accordion title="A signer says they didn't receive the signature request email">
    Ask the signer to check their spam or junk folder, as signature request emails from DocuSign or CertEurope can occasionally be filtered. If the email isn't there, go to the **Signatures** dashboard within your Closd project, locate the pending request, and use the **Resend** option to send the notification again. Before resending, double-check that the email address entered for that signer is correct — if it contains a typo, you may need to void the current request and create a new one with the corrected address.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A signer is having trouble completing the signing process on their device">
    Advise the signer to use a modern, up-to-date browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari are all supported. Ask them to clear their browser cache and cookies and then reopen the signature link. If they are signing on a mobile device and continue to experience issues, suggest they try the same link on a desktop or laptop browser instead, particularly for Qualified Electronic Signature workflows that may involve identity verification steps requiring more screen space or camera access. Extensions and aggressive privacy settings in some browsers can also interfere with signature workflows — trying an incognito/private window can help isolate this.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The signature request expired before all signers completed it">
    Once a signature request expires, it is void and cannot be reactivated. You will need to create a new signature request for the same document from the **Signatures** section of your project. When setting up the new request, consider extending the expiry deadline to give all signers adequate time. It is also good practice to send a direct reminder to any outstanding signers a few days before the deadline — Closd's signature dashboard lets you send reminders without voiding the original request.

    <Info>
      To avoid expiry issues on time-sensitive closings, send signature requests as early as possible in the process and use Closd's reminder functionality to nudge signers proactively.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

</AccordionGroup>

## Still Need Help?

If you've worked through the relevant steps above and are still experiencing an issue, the Closd support team is ready to help. You can reach support directly via the **in-app chat** — click the help icon in the bottom corner of any Closd screen — or contact your dedicated account manager if you are on an enterprise plan. When reaching out, have your project name, the email address on your account, and a brief description of the issue ready to speed up the resolution process.
