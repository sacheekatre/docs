Closd is built for collaboration across the full deal team. You can invite internal colleagues from your firm or organization, as well as external parties such as counterparties, advisors, notaries, and banks — all within the same secure project environment. Access is controlled through roles, so each participant sees and can do exactly what they need, nothing more.

---

## Roles and Permissions

Every member of a Closd project is assigned a role that defines what they can see and do. Assign roles carefully based on each person's responsibilities in the transaction.

| Role | What They Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Administrator** | Full access to all features. Can add and remove members, change project and account settings, and manage billing. |
| **Project Manager** | Can create and manage projects, organize checklists, and invite team members to projects they oversee. |
| **Editor** | Can upload and manage documents, complete checklist items, and sign documents when requested. Cannot change project settings or manage members. |
| **Viewer** | Read-only access. Can view documents and monitor checklist status, but cannot upload, edit, or sign. |

<Tip>
  When inviting external parties — such as a counterparty's counsel or a financing bank — use the **Viewer** or **Editor** role as appropriate. External guests do not need a paid Closd subscription to participate in a project you share with them.
</Tip>

## Inviting Someone to a Project

You can add a new member to any project where you have sufficient permissions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the project">
    From your Closd dashboard, click on the project you want to add someone to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to the Members tab">
    Inside the project, navigate to the **Members** tab in the project navigation menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Member">
    Click the **Add Member** button. A panel appears where you can enter the new member's details.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter their email address">
    Type the email address of the person you want to invite. If they already have a Closd account, their name may appear as a suggestion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a role">
    Select the appropriate role from the dropdown — **Administrator**, **Project Manager**, **Editor**, or **Viewer** — based on their responsibilities in the transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the invitation">
    Click **Send Invitation**. The invitee receives an email with a link to join the project. If they don't yet have a Closd account, the link guides them through a lightweight registration process.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Removing a Member

If someone's involvement in a project ends — for example, after a deal closes or if a team member changes — you can revoke their access at any time.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Members tab">
    Open the relevant project and click the **Members** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the member">
    Locate the person you want to remove from the member list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove their access">
    Click the options menu (⋯) next to their name and select **Remove Member**. Confirm the action when prompted.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Removing a member revokes their access **immediately**. They will no longer be able to view the project, any documents within it, or any materials they previously had access to. Make sure the person has downloaded or saved anything they legitimately need before you remove them.
</Warning>
