A closing binder — sometimes called a transaction bible — is the definitive record of a completed deal: every signed document, organized and packaged for all parties to retain. Traditionally, assembling one meant days of manual sorting, printing, and collating. Closd automates the entire process, compiling a polished PDF package in minutes directly from your project.

## What's Included

The closing binder draws directly from your project checklist, so every document is already organized the way your team structured it. You can tailor the contents to suit the deal and the audience.

A closing binder can include:

- **All signed documents** from the project, ordered according to your checklist structure
- **A cover page** with the deal name, closing date, and parties
- **A table of contents** automatically generated from your document list
- **A deal summary sheet** for a high-level overview of the transaction
- **Signature pages** compiled and presented clearly for each document

You can check or uncheck individual items before generating, giving you full control over what appears in the final package.

## Generate a Closing Binder

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Closing tab">
    From your project, click **Closing** in the top navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start the generation">
    Click **Generate Closing Binder** to open the configuration panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the document list">
    A list of all project documents is displayed. Check or uncheck items to include or exclude them from the binder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize the cover page">
    Enter or confirm the deal name, closing date, and party names. These details appear on the binder's cover page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your output format">
    Select **Single merged PDF** for one consolidated file, or **Zip of individual files** if parties prefer documents delivered separately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate the binder">
    Click **Generate**. Closd compiles all selected documents and assembles the binder — this typically takes one to five minutes for large binders.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download and distribute">
    Once ready, download the binder from the Closing tab and share it with all parties.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Generate a draft closing binder a few days before the scheduled closing date. This lets you catch any missing signatures, absent documents, or checklist gaps while there is still time to resolve them.
</Tip>

## Sharing the Closing Binder

Once generated, you can share the closing binder directly within Closd via a secure link — no email attachment required. All project members with access to the Closing tab can view and download the binder at any time.

If you prefer to distribute the binder outside the platform, download it and send it by email or upload it to your preferred document management system. The PDF is self-contained and requires no Closd account to open.

<Note>
  The closing binder is a snapshot of your project at the moment it was generated. If any documents are signed, replaced, or updated after generation, those changes will not be reflected automatically — you will need to generate a new binder to capture the latest version.
</Note>
