Paper originals have long been the gold standard for legal admissibility — but maintaining physical archives is costly, fragile, and impractical for modern transactions. Closd's Electronic Safe provides the digital equivalent: a certified long-term archive where your signed contracts carry the same legal weight as paper originals, backed by a tamper-proof chain of custody.

## What Is Probative Value?

A document with probative value is legally admissible as evidence in court. It proves two things: that the document is authentic, and that it has not been altered since it was archived. Without certified archiving, a digital file can be questioned — its integrity is impossible to guarantee without technical controls.

Closd's Electronic Safe achieves probative value through its partnership with **Arkhineo**, a leading European digital archiving provider. Every archived document receives an integrity certificate and a complete chain of custody, making it the legal equivalent of a paper original under French and EU law.

<Note>
  Arkhineo is certified under **NF Z42-013** (the French standard for trusted digital archiving) and complies with **eIDAS**, the EU regulation on electronic identification and trust services. This certification ensures your archives meet the highest legal standards for digital originals across France and the European Union.
</Note>

## Setting Up the Electronic Safe

The Electronic Safe is available as a module within your Closd subscription. If it is not yet active for your organization, you will need to enable it before archiving documents.

To activate the Electronic Safe:

1. Contact your **Closd account manager** or reach out to [Closd support](https://app.closd.com) to request activation.
2. Your account manager will confirm whether the module is included in your current plan or requires a separate subscription.
3. Once activated, the **Electronic Safe** option will appear in your project Documents tab for eligible signed documents.

## Archiving a Document

Once the Electronic Safe is active for your organization, you can archive any signed document directly from your project.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the Documents tab">
    Open your project and navigate to the **Documents** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the document to archive">
    Find the signed document you want to archive. Click on it to open its detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to the Electronic Safe">
    Click **Archive to Electronic Safe**. A confirmation panel will appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the retention period">
    Choose the appropriate retention period for this document type — for example, 10 years or 30 years. See the [Retention Periods](#retention-periods) section below for guidance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit** to confirm. Closd sends the document to Arkhineo's certified archive. A **certificate of deposit** is issued and attached to the document record, confirming the archiving date, document identity, and integrity fingerprint.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Once a document has been archived in the Electronic Safe, it cannot be deleted before its retention period expires. This restriction is intentional — it is what guarantees the document's integrity and legal admissibility. Plan your archiving decisions carefully before submitting.
</Info>

## Retrieving Archived Documents

You can access your archived documents at any time through Closd, subject to your access permissions.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the Electronic Safe">
    From your project or your organization dashboard, open the **Electronic Safe** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the document">
    Browse or search for the document you need. Documents are listed with their archiving date and retention period.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the archive package">
    Click **Download**. You receive the original signed file along with its **integrity certificate** — a cryptographic proof that the document has not been modified since it was archived.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Access to the Electronic Safe can be restricted to authorized users only. Contact your account manager to configure access controls for your organization.

## Retention Periods

Different document types carry different legal retention requirements. When archiving, you set the retention period once — and the document is guaranteed to remain in the archive for that entire duration.

Common retention periods include:

| Document Type | Recommended Retention |
|---|---|
| Commercial contracts (France) | 10 years |
| Real estate deeds (France) | 30 years |
| Corporate resolutions | 10 years |
| Loan agreements | 30 years (or duration of loan + statutory period) |

You can also configure a custom retention period if your jurisdiction or internal policy requires it.

<Tip>
  Consult your legal counsel before setting retention periods. The appropriate duration depends on the document type, the governing law, and any sector-specific regulations that apply to your organization. Setting too short a retention period could leave you without a legally admissible record when you need it most.
</Tip>
