During due diligence, buyers and their advisors typically generate a high volume of questions for the sell-side team. When those questions are handled by email, threads multiply, answers get lost, and nothing is easy to audit at closing. Closd's Q&A module replaces fragmented email communications with a single, structured workflow — every question is submitted, assigned, answered, and logged in one place.

## How It Works

Buyers and their advisors submit questions directly through the data room. The sell-side team receives each question in a central inbox, can assign it to the right person for a response, and submits the answer through the same interface. Every question, assignment, and answer is timestamped and attributed to the individual who performed each action.

The Q&A module is role-based: buy-side participants can submit questions and view answers addressed to them, while sell-side administrators have full visibility across all threads and can control which answers are visible to which bidders.

## Submitting a Question

As a buyer or buy-side advisor, follow these steps to submit a question through the Q&A module:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Q&A tab">
    Inside the data room, click the **Q&A** tab in the top navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create a new question">
    Click **New Question** to open the question form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a category">
    Choose the most appropriate category from the dropdown (for example, "Financial", "Legal", "Tax", "Commercial", or "Other"). Categories help the sell-side team route questions to the right expert quickly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your question">
    Type your question in the text field. Be as specific as possible — precise questions receive faster, more accurate answers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reference a document (optional)">
    If your question relates to a specific document in the data room, use the document reference field to link to it. This gives the answering party immediate context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit">
    Click **Submit**. Your question will appear in the Q&A log with an **Open** status and will be visible to the sell-side administrators.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Answering Questions

As a sell-side administrator or assigned respondent, follow these steps to answer a submitted question:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Q&A tab">
    Navigate to the **Q&A** tab in your data room. Questions requiring a response will appear with an **Open** or **In Progress** status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the question">
    Click on the question you want to answer to open the detail view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign if needed">
    If the question should be handled by a colleague, assign it to them before answering. Assigned questions move to **In Progress** status.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Answer">
    Click **Answer** to open the response form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type your response">
    Write your answer in the response field. You can format the text for clarity.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach a document (optional)">
    If your answer is best supported by a document, attach it directly to the response. The attached file becomes accessible to the recipient through the Q&A thread.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set visibility">
    Choose whether the answer is visible to **All Bidders** or only to **The Asking Party**. Use "All Bidders" for answers that clarify factual matters relevant to everyone; use "Asking Party Only" for commercially sensitive responses.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit the answer">
    Click **Submit Answer**. The question status will update to **Answered** and the relevant party or parties will be notified.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Q&A Statuses

Every question moves through a defined lifecycle. Understanding each status helps you manage your process and identify bottlenecks at a glance.

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Open** | The question has been submitted and is awaiting assignment or a response from the sell-side team. |
| **In Progress** | The question has been assigned to a team member and a response is being prepared. |
| **Answered** | The sell-side team has submitted a response. The asking party can review the answer in the Q&A tab. |
| **Closed** | The question has been resolved and no further action is expected. Administrators can close questions manually once they are satisfied the matter is settled. |

<Tip>
  Set a clear question deadline early in the process and communicate it to all buy-side participants. Informing bidders of the last date on which new questions will be accepted gives your team predictable workload, prevents last-minute question floods, and signals a well-run process to all parties.
</Tip>

<Note>
  At the conclusion of the due diligence process, you can export the entire Q&A log — including all questions, answers, attachments, and timestamps — as a PDF or Excel file. This export is useful as a closing deliverable, for post-deal records, or in the event of a dispute about what was disclosed and when.
</Note>
