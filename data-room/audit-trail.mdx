In M&A and due diligence, knowing exactly who saw which documents — and when — is not just a nice-to-have. It is essential for accountability, regulatory compliance, and post-deal protection. If a leak occurs, or if a party later disputes what information was made available to them, a reliable audit trail is your first line of defence. Closd automatically records every significant action taken in your data room, so you always have a complete, accurate record without any manual effort.

## What Is Logged

The Closd audit trail captures a comprehensive set of user actions. Every logged event includes the name and email address of the user who performed the action, the date and time, and — where applicable — the specific document or folder involved.

| Action | Details Recorded |
|---|---|
| **Document viewed** | User, timestamp, device type |
| **Document downloaded** | User, timestamp, document name |
| **Document printed** | User, timestamp, document name |
| **User logged in** | User, timestamp, IP address |
| **User logged out** | User, timestamp |
| **Permission changes** | Administrator who made the change, affected user or group, old and new permission level, timestamp |
| **Q&A question submitted** | Submitting user, question category, timestamp |
| **Q&A question answered** | Responding user, visibility setting applied, timestamp |
| **File uploaded** | Uploading user, file name, destination folder, timestamp |
| **File deleted** | User, file name, timestamp |

<Info>
  The audit log is read-only and cannot be altered or deleted by any user, including data room administrators. This tamper-proof design ensures the log is reliable as evidence for compliance purposes or in the event of a dispute.
</Info>

## Viewing the Audit Trail

You can access the full audit log at any point during or after the due diligence process. To view the audit trail:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Data Room">
    Navigate to the Data Room within your Closd project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Activity">
    Click **Activity** in the data room navigation bar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the Audit Log">
    Select **Audit Log** from the Activity menu. The full log will load in reverse chronological order — most recent actions first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Filter the results">
    Use the filter options to narrow the log by **User**, **Date Range**, or **Action Type**. For example, filter by a specific user to see everything they accessed, or filter by "Document downloaded" to review all download activity at a glance.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exporting the Audit Log

You may need to share the audit log with counterparties, regulators, or your own compliance team at the conclusion of a deal. Closd lets you export the full log or any filtered view in two formats.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Apply any desired filters">
    Before exporting, use the filter panel to scope the export to the records you need — for example, a specific date range or a specific user group.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Export">
    Click the **Export** button in the top-right corner of the Audit Log screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your format">
    Select **CSV** for a machine-readable spreadsheet that can be imported into Excel or other tools, or **PDF** for a formatted report suitable for submission or filing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the file">
    The file will generate and download automatically. Save it to your deal records.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Export the full, unfiltered audit log at closing and include it in your closing deliverables. It provides a permanent record of every participant's access and activity throughout the due diligence process — valuable protection for all parties long after the transaction has completed.
</Tip>
