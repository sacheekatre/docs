Closd brings electronic signature directly into your transaction workflow, so you can send, sign, and store documents without ever leaving your project. Whether you're closing an M&A deal, executing employment contracts, or filing regulated documents, Closd supports the right level of signature for every situation — all compliant with EU eIDAS regulations.

## Signature Types

Closd supports three levels of electronic signature, each suited to different document types and legal requirements. Click a card below to learn which type is right for your transaction.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Simple Signature (SES)" icon="pen" href="/esignature/signature-types">
    Click-to-sign with no identity verification. Legally valid for most routine commercial agreements, NDAs, and board resolutions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Advanced Signature (AES)" icon="shield-halved" href="/esignature/signature-types">
    Identity verified via email and SMS. Suitable for employment contracts, commercial leases, and agreements requiring higher assurance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Qualified Signature (QES)" icon="certificate" href="/esignature/signature-types">
    The highest EU legal standard — equivalent to a handwritten signature. Required for notarized deeds, real estate transfers, and regulated filings.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## How It Works

You initiate a signature request directly from a document in your Closd project — no need to switch to a separate application. Once sent, each signer receives an email invitation with a secure link. You can track exactly who has signed, who is pending, and who has declined, all in real time from your project checklist.

The entire process — from sending to storing the final executed document — happens within Closd. This keeps your transaction timeline and document record in one place throughout the deal lifecycle.

## Supported Providers

Closd integrates with two trusted e-signature providers:

- **DocuSign** — The world's most widely used e-signature platform. Ideal for Simple and Advanced signatures on cross-border transactions where broad market familiarity is important.
- **CertEurope** — A French qualified trust service provider (QTSP) accredited under eIDAS. Required for Qualified Electronic Signatures (QES), particularly for French-law documents, notarized deeds, and regulated filings that demand the highest legal standard.

Your project configuration determines which provider is used. Contact your Closd administrator if you are unsure which provider applies to your transaction.

## For Signers

Signers do **not** need a Closd account to sign a document. They receive a secure email link that opens the document in their browser — no software to install and no registration required. The signing experience is fully mobile-compatible, so signers can complete their signature from any device.

<Note>
  Once all parties have signed, the fully executed document is automatically saved back into your project's document library with a qualified timestamp. You do not need to upload it manually — Closd handles the storage for you.
</Note>
