You can send any document for electronic signature directly from your Closd project in a few steps. The entire process — from selecting the document to tracking who has signed — stays within your transaction workspace, so nothing falls through the cracks.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select the document">
    Open your project and navigate to the **Documents** tab. Click on the document you want to send for signature, then select **Send for Signature** from the document menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose the signature type">
    Select **Simple**, **Advanced**, or **Qualified** depending on the legal requirements of your document. If you are unsure which level to use, refer to [Choosing the Right Signature Type](/esignature/signature-types) for guidance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add signers">
    Enter each signer's name and email address. You can add as many signers as needed. Choose whether signers should sign **sequentially** (one after another in a defined order) or **in parallel** (all at the same time). Sequential order is common for documents that require a chain of approvals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place signature fields">
    The document opens in the signature editor. Drag and drop **signature**, **initials**, **date**, and any other required fields onto the correct pages and positions. Assign each field to the appropriate signer to ensure the right person signs in the right place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a message">
    Optionally, write a personal message that signers will see in their invitation email. Use this to provide context, instructions, or a signing deadline reminder for the other parties.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send">
    Click **Send for Signature**. Each signer immediately receives an email invitation containing a secure link to review and sign the document. No Closd account is required on their end.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Set a signing deadline when you send the request. Once the deadline passes, the signature request expires automatically and all pending signers are notified. This is especially useful for time-sensitive closings or regulatory filings.
</Tip>

## Tracking Signing Status

After sending, you can monitor progress without leaving your project. The checklist item linked to the document updates in real time to reflect the current signing status. For a full breakdown, open the **Signatures** dashboard, where you can see at a glance:

- Which signers have **completed** their signature
- Which signers are still **pending**
- Whether any signer has **declined** to sign

This visibility helps you follow up quickly and keep the transaction on schedule.

## Reminders and Cancellation

If a signer has not yet completed their signature, you can send them a **manual reminder** directly from the Signatures dashboard. A new email notification is sent to the pending signer with their original secure link.

If circumstances change and you need to stop the process, you can **cancel** the signature request at any time. Cancelling voids the request and automatically notifies all signers that the request is no longer valid.

<Warning>
  Once all parties have signed, the document is locked and cannot be edited. If any amendment is required after full execution, you must upload the revised document and initiate a new signature request.
</Warning>
