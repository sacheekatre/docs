Closd's checklists replace the Word and Excel step plans that legal teams have traditionally passed around by email. Instead of maintaining a static spreadsheet, your entire team works from a single, live checklist — every update is visible in real time, every task has a clear owner, and nothing falls through the cracks.

## Adding a Checklist Item

Each item on your checklist represents a discrete task in the transaction — for example, *"Obtain board approval"* or *"Deliver executed SPA to notary"*.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Checklist Tab">
    Navigate to your project and click the **Checklist** tab at the top of the project workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Add Item">
    Click the **+ Add Item** button at the bottom of a section, or use the toolbar to insert a new item at a specific position within the list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a Title">
    Type a clear, action-oriented title for the task. Good checklist items begin with a verb — for example, *"Draft shareholders' agreement"* or *"Confirm regulatory filings submitted"*.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign to a Team Member">
    Use the **Assignee** field to select the person responsible for completing this item. Only project members appear in the dropdown. You can assign multiple people to a single item if shared ownership is needed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a Due Date">
    Enter the date by which this item must be completed. Due dates feed into Closd's automatic reminder system and are visible in the checklist calendar view.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Attach a Document (Optional)">
    If this task relates to a specific document — such as a draft agreement or a regulatory form — click **Attach Document** to link a file from the project's document library. The attachment appears directly on the checklist item for easy reference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the Item">
    Click **Save**. The item is added to the checklist and is immediately visible to all project members with access.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Organising Items into Sections

For any transaction of real complexity, a flat list of tasks quickly becomes hard to navigate. Closd lets you group related items into **sections** — named categories that act as collapsible headings within your checklist.

Common sections for a typical M&A transaction include:

- **Due Diligence** — document requests, review tasks, Q&A items
- **Documentation** — drafting, negotiation, and execution of transaction documents
- **Signing** — execution logistics, signature pages, execution versions
- **Post-Closing** — filings, payments, notifications, and housekeeping

To add a section, click **+ Add Section** and give it a name. You can drag and drop both sections and items to reorder them as your transaction evolves.

## Tracking Progress

The **progress bar** at the top of the Checklist tab shows the percentage of items marked as completed across the entire project. It updates instantly whenever an item's status changes, giving every team member a live snapshot of where the deal stands.

Each checklist item carries one of four statuses:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Not Started** | The task has not been picked up yet |
| **In Progress** | The assignee is actively working on this item |
| **Completed** | The task is done and no further action is needed |
| **Blocked** | The task cannot proceed — typically waiting on a third party or a preceding task |

To update a status, click on the item and select the appropriate value from the status dropdown.

<Tip>
  Use the **Blocked** status — and add a comment explaining why — whenever an item is waiting on an external party such as a counterparty's counsel, a regulator, or a notary. This makes it immediately clear to the whole team that the delay is external, and creates a transparent audit trail of the hold-up.
</Tip>

## Automatic Reminders

Closd automatically sends **email reminders** to assignees as their due dates approach, so you don't need to chase people manually. By default, reminders are sent one day before and on the day of the due date.

To adjust the reminder schedule for a specific item:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Item">
    Click on the checklist item to open its detail panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go to Reminders">
    Click the **Reminders** option within the item settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the Schedule">
    Choose when reminders should be sent — for example, seven days before, three days before, and on the day. You can enable multiple reminders for the same item.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save Your Changes">
    Click **Save**. The assignee will receive email notifications at each of the times you have configured.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Project administrators can also set project-wide default reminder preferences from the project settings, applying a consistent reminder schedule to all newly created checklist items.

## Using Checklist Templates

Checklist templates let you reuse a proven task structure across multiple transactions of the same type, without rebuilding it from scratch each time.

**To apply a template to your project:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Checklist Tab">
    Navigate to the **Checklist** tab in your project.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Import Template">
    Click the **Import Template** button in the checklist toolbar. Your library of available templates — including Closd's built-in templates and any you have saved — will appear.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a Template">
    Choose the template you want to apply. You can preview its sections and items before confirming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Apply**. The template's sections and items are added to your checklist. You can then adjust, rename, or delete items to fit your specific transaction.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**To save your current checklist as a template:**

Open the checklist toolbar, click **Save as Template**, and give it a descriptive name — for example, *"Standard M&A Checklist – French Law"*. The template is saved to your library and can be applied to any future project.

<Note>
  Every change made to a checklist — adding items, updating statuses, attaching documents, or leaving comments — is automatically logged in the project's **Activity feed**. This provides a full, timestamped audit trail of all actions taken throughout the life of the transaction.
</Note>
