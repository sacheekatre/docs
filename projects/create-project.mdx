Every transaction in Closd lives inside a **project** — a dedicated workspace that brings together your checklists, documents, team members, and activity in one place. Creating a project takes only a few minutes, and once it's set up you can immediately start adding tasks, uploading documents, and inviting collaborators.

## Creating a New Project

Navigate to your Closd dashboard at [app.closd.com](https://app.closd.com) and follow the steps below to get your deal workspace ready.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click New Project">
    From your dashboard, click the **New Project** button in the top-right corner. This opens the project creation wizard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter a Project Name">
    Type a clear, descriptive name for your project — for example, *"Acquisition of Acme Corp"*. A good name makes the project easy to identify in your project list, especially when you have many active deals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the Transaction Type">
    Choose the category that best matches your deal:
    - **M&A** — mergers, acquisitions, and corporate restructurings
    - **Real Estate** — property purchases, sales, and financing
    - **Private Equity** — fund investments and portfolio transactions
    - **Banking & Finance** — loan facilities, bonds, and structured finance
    - **Other** — any transaction that doesn't fit the categories above

    The transaction type helps Closd suggest relevant templates and default checklist structures.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the Expected Closing Date">
    Enter the anticipated closing date for your transaction. This step is optional, but setting a date enables deadline-based reminders and helps the team stay oriented around the timeline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a Starting Point">
    Decide whether to:
    - **Start from a blank project** — a clean workspace with no pre-loaded content
    - **Use an existing template** — a project pre-populated with a checklist structure and folder layout suited to your transaction type

    If you have previously saved a project as a template, it will appear in your template library here.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Create Project">
    Click **Create Project** to confirm. Closd creates your project and assigns you as the **project administrator**, giving you full control over settings, members, and permissions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Name your projects with enough detail to be identifiable at a glance. Prefer *"Acquisition of Acme Corp – 2024"* over simply *"Acme"* — you'll thank yourself when searching through a long project list months later.
</Tip>

## Using Templates

Templates save you significant setup time on recurring transaction types. Instead of rebuilding a checklist and folder structure from scratch for every deal, you can apply a template and start with a proven framework already in place.

Closd provides a library of **built-in templates** covering common transaction types such as M&A, real estate closings, and private equity investments. You can also **save any existing project as a template**: open the project settings, select *Save as Template*, give it a name, and it will appear in your template library for future use.

When you apply a template, all checklist items, sections, and folder structures are copied into the new project. You can then customise them freely — adding, removing, or renaming items — without affecting the original template.

## Project Dashboard

Once your project is created, you land on the **project dashboard**. Here is a quick overview of what you will find:

| Tab | What It Contains |
|---|---|
| **Checklist** | Your interactive transaction checklist — tasks, assignees, due dates, and completion status |
| **Documents** | The centralised document library for the project, organised in folders |
| **Members** | The list of all users who have access to the project and their roles |
| **Activity** | A real-time feed of all actions taken in the project — uploads, status changes, comments, and more |

Start by inviting your team members from the **Members** tab, then move on to setting up your checklist and uploading your first documents.
